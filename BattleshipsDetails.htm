The water simulation used here was designed to work on a range of hardware, some of which is dated now. For this reason it wasn't implemented using shaders. Refraction and environment mapping was used with simple blending to create the effect. The reflection texture was also moved using the texture matrix in order to create sky movement over the sea. Ripple effects are seen around the area shots are fired. These effects were created by propargating forces through the buffer using a filter (similar to that which you would use in image processing).

Aircraft were simulated to be flying over head by blending shadows and clipping to the water area. Bonuses can be picked up to help players with questions or to help them find ships. A number of bonuses are also awarded at the end of the game. Amongst the bonuses were sonars which allow a player to scan an area of water for ships. This effect required blending a section of water in a circle shape, at the same time as a scanner line moved in a circle.

The game-over sequence was pre-rendered in bink format by an artist, along with the movie seen after the entire fleet has been sunk.

Overall this is a project which I enjoyed developing the most due to the extra effects and work involved in rendering the water. Explosions could have done with some better blending when multiple hits sunk a ship,
